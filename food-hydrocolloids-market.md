<p><p>The food hydrocolloids market comprises various types, each serving distinct functionalities in food applications. Agar is a gelatinous substance used for thickening and gelling. Alginates, derived from algae, act as stabilizers and thickeners. Carboxymethylcellulose and other cellulose ethers enhance viscosity. Carrageenan lends texture and stability. Gelatin provides structure and mouthfeel, while gellan gum offers gel-forming properties. Guar gum and gum acacia contribute to viscosity and emulsification, and locust bean gum serves as a thickener, creating a diverse range of functional ingredients in food products.</p></p>
