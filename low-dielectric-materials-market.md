<p><p>The low dielectric materials market comprises three main types: thermoplastic, thermoset, and ceramics. Thermoplastics are flexible polymers that can be reprocessed when heated, making them ideal for various applications. Thermosets, on the other hand, are rigid polymers that undergo permanent curing, offering superior thermal and chemical resistance. Ceramics are inorganic, non-metallic materials known for their high thermal stability and low dielectric constant, making them suitable for high-frequency applications in electronics and telecommunications. Each type serves distinct needs in various industries.</p></p>
