<p><p>The Lyme Disease diagnostics market features several key testing types. Serological tests detect antibodies produced in response to Lyme infection, while urine antigen tests identify specific proteins from the bacteria. Lymphocytic transformation tests assess immune response by measuring lymphocyte activation. Immunofluorescent staining uses fluorescently labeled antibodies to visualize Lyme bacteria in samples. Nucleic acid tests, such as PCR, amplify the pathogen's genetic material for precise detection. Each method offers unique advantages in diagnosing and managing Lyme Disease effectively.</p></p>
